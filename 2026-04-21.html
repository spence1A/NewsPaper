Study: The Atlantic Current Could Weaken Up to 58% by 2100 — Far Worse Than Feared

A peer-reviewed study published April 15 in Science Advances found that the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) — the ocean conveyor belt that regulates climate across the Northern Hemisphere — is likely to weaken by 42 to 58% by 2100. That's significantly more severe than prior estimates. The most realistic climate models indicate a high probability of eventual collapse, which would bring colder winters to Europe and more extreme weather along the U.S. East Coast.
