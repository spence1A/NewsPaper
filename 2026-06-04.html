Researchers have discovered a method to reverse the direction of energy flow in turbulence, challenging a fundamental theory of fluid dynamics that has stood for more than eight decades. In classical turbulence theory, energy cascades from large eddies down to smaller ones — but the new work demonstrates the opposite cascade is achievable under specific conditions. The finding has potential implications for energy efficiency in engineering and fluid-based computing systems.
