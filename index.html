252,757 miles. A new human record.

NASA's Artemis II crewed lunar flyby — the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in 1972 — broke the record for the furthest humans have ever been from Earth as Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen looped the far side of the Moon. It is, quietly, the opening act of the next half-century of human spaceflight.
