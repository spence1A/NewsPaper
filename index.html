James Webb Found a Planet That Shouldn't Exist

The James Webb Space Telescope identified TOI-5205 b — an exoplanet whose atmosphere defies every current model of planetary formation. It orbits a small M-dwarf star but is too large and gas-rich for its host's mass, earning the nickname "the forbidden planet." Scientists are scrambling to update formation theories, because standard models simply can't produce what JWST is observing. It's the latest in a string of discoveries quietly dismantling textbook planetary science.

A New Registry Is Mapping Who Actually Uses Differential Privacy

A collaborative research team launched the Differential Privacy Deployments Registry — a public database tracking which companies and government agencies are actively using differential privacy, a mathematical technique that lets you learn from data without exposing individuals. The initiative was born from frustration that DP was being claimed widely but deployed rarely. Apple, Google, and the US Census Bureau are among the verified users. As AI data appetite grows, knowing which platforms actually protect their training data mathematically is becoming a meaningful trust signal.
