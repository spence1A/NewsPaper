Privacy Law · May 11

GM pays $12.75M — the largest CCPA penalty ever — to settle driver-data sale claims.

California AG Rob Bonta led the action against GM's sale of geolocation and driver-behavior data to consumer reporting agencies, the kind of telemetry that quietly underpinned insurance scoring and "surge"-style premium increases. The settlement halts the sale entirely and sets a new ceiling for state privacy enforcement.

Science · May 7

Researchers stumble on DNA that breaks the rules of life.

A routine single-cell sequencing run on a microscopic pond organism turned up a genetic code that doesn't follow the near-universal "rules" — rewriting, of all things, how genes signal their own end. The find scrambles a handful of textbook assumptions about translation, codon usage, and the boundaries of the genetic alphabet.

Physics · May 4

Scientists create exotic forms of matter that shouldn't exist.

A team led by Cal Poly's Ian Powell drove materials with carefully timed magnetic shifts — "Flux-Switching Floquet Engineering" — and coaxed them into quantum states more stable and error-resistant than anything seen at this scale. The result, published in Physical Review B, points to a cleaner path toward fault-tolerant quantum computing.
