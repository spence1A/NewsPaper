The lawsuit argues the company violated the California Consumer Privacy Act by neglecting to protect extraordinarily sensitive biological information. NASA's X-59 Supersonic Jet Prepares to Break the Sound Barrier for the First Time

NASA's experimental X-59 "quiet" supersonic aircraft is about to face its biggest test yet: breaking the sound barrier in live flight. The jet is designed to produce a soft "thump" rather than the disruptive sonic boom that banned commercial supersonic flight over land decades ago. If the test succeeds and community noise surveys support it, the data will be submitted to regulators as the scientific basis for revising supersonic overland rules — potentially opening the door to a new era of high-speed commercial aviation.

NASA's Roman Telescope Could Discover 100,000 New Exoplanets — More Than All Prior Missions Combined

New analysis suggests NASA's Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope has the potential to detect approximately 100,000 exoplanets through gravitational microlensing — a number that would dwarf every previous mission's combined haul. Unlike transit-detection methods, microlensing can find cold, distant planets and free-floating rogue worlds invisible to other telescopes. Scientists say Roman could fundamentally reshape the statistical picture of what types of planetary systems exist in the galaxy.
