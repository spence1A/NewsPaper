Astronomy · Discovery

Astronomers Finally Crack the Mystery of Repeating Fast Radio Bursts

A new study published in June has resolved one of radio astronomy's most puzzling open questions: why a subset of fast radio bursts (FRBs) repeat while the majority fire only once. Scientists have identified a structural difference in the source environments of repeating versus non-repeating FRBs, providing the strongest evidence yet for two distinct physical mechanisms. Fast radio bursts last mere milliseconds but release more energy than the Sun does in days — and the ability to predict which ones will repeat could transform them into precision cosmological tools for mapping the universe's missing matter.

Scientists ID the Protein That Causes CAR T-Cell Cancer Therapy to Burn Out

Researchers have identified a protein called NFIL3 as the key driver behind why CAR T-cell therapies lose their cancer-fighting effectiveness over time — a major obstacle that has limited the treatments to blood cancers and prevented durable remissions in some patients. By understanding the molecular mechanism behind T-cell exhaustion, scientists now have a specific target for next-generation therapies that could remain active longer and potentially extend CAR T's reach to solid tumors. Clinical trials testing NFIL3 inhibition strategies are expected to begin within the next 18 months.
