The research is early-stage but represents one of the most significant steps yet toward genuine human-machine neural integration.

Mars Had a Monster Sandstorm 3 Billion Years Ago — And We Just Found the Proof

A serendipitous observation by the Curiosity rover has revealed ripple marks preserved in Gale Crater's rock layers that could only have been formed by an exceptionally powerful sandstorm roughly three billion years ago. Published in the journal Geology, the findings suggest Mars experienced extreme weather events during a period scientists previously considered relatively calm. The discovery rewrites the timeline of Martian atmospheric history and raises new questions about what else might be preserved in the planet's ancient rock record.

Scientists Finally Grew Dolomite in a Lab After 200 Years of Trying

Dolomite — one of the most abundant minerals in Earth's geological record — has puzzled scientists since the 1700s because no one could reproduce its formation under normal lab conditions. After two centuries of failed attempts, researchers have finally cracked the mystery by replicating the precise combination of microbial activity, temperature, and chemical environment that allows dolomite to crystallize. The breakthrough resolves one of geology's longest-standing enigmas and could reshape how we model ancient ocean chemistry and carbon cycling on Earth.
