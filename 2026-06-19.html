Surveillance · June

Hackers breach Tchap, the French government's encrypted messenger.

France's digital affairs directorate warned that attackers hijacked a single user account via social engineering to breach Tchap, the government's encrypted messaging service. The intruder scraped every channel that account belonged to, claiming 13.5GB of data from the tax authority and other civil servants.

UV light breaks down “forever chemicals” with no added reagents.

Researchers showed that hydrogen radicals generated by intense ultraviolet light can break apart stubborn PFAS “forever chemicals” without any added chemicals. It's a potentially cleaner, simpler route to destroying a class of pollutants that has resisted conventional treatment for decades.

Southern California's fault system is the most stressed in 1,000 years.

A new study finds Southern California's major fault system is carrying more accumulated stress than at any point in the last millennium. The result sharpens long-running questions about regional earthquake risk and how much strain the system can hold before it releases.

Science · June 17

Sculpting a surface keeps an ultrathin superconductor working harder.

Researchers in Sweden found that subtly sculpting the surface beneath an ultrathin superconducting film lets it stay superconducting at higher temperatures and under far stronger magnetic fields. The trick points toward more robust superconductors without exotic new materials.
