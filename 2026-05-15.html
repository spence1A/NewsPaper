Meta Will Scan Your Body to Guess Your Age

Meta announced it will deploy AI to analyze height, bone structure, and other physical cues in user photos and videos to determine whether accounts belong to minors under 13. If the system flags a likely underage user, Meta will deactivate the account and require age verification before reinstatement. The move is a response to mounting regulatory pressure around child safety on Instagram and Facebook, though privacy advocates are already raising concerns about biometric surveillance being baked into routine social media use.

The Bill That Could Kill 21 State Privacy Laws at Once

The Electronic Frontier Foundation published a sharp critique this week of the SECURE Data Act, flagging that Section 15 of the bill would preempt any state law, rule, or regulation that "relates to" its provisions — effectively wiping out 21 state consumer privacy laws in a single stroke. Critics argue the bill, framed as a federal baseline, is in practice a rollback disguised as progress, stripping protections that states like California and Virginia have spent years building. The bill is still moving through committee, and its final scope remains contested.

Astronomy · AI Science

AI Uncovers 10,000 Hidden Planets in NASA's Archives

Scientists used a new machine-learning model called RAVEN to sweep through light-curve data from NASA's TESS mission, scanning 83.7 million stars in the process. The result: 10,091 new exoplanet candidates that had never been identified before — potentially tripling the known count of alien worlds — including bizarre "hot Neptunes" orbiting their stars in under a day and planets lurking in the theoretically depleted Neptunian desert. Separately, the AI tool confirmed over 100 previously unverified exoplanets, with 31 representing brand-new worlds. The findings show that most of the TESS archive's faint stars had never been seriously analyzed before now.

Scientists Find DNA That Breaks the Rules of Life

Researchers using a new single-cell DNA sequencing method made an accidental discovery that is rippling through the biology community: a microscopic pond organism appears to use a genetic code that diverges from the near-universal rules all known life follows. The find, published in early May, challenges foundational assumptions about how genetic information is read and expressed at the cellular level. Scientists caution the organism is an extreme outlier, but the discovery raises fresh questions about how many other "rule-breaking" life forms may be hiding in overlooked microbial corners of the planet.
