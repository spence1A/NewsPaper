YouTube quietly reshapes brand deals into one pipeline.

YouTube's newly named Creator Partnerships platform — the old BrandConnect, now wired into Studio, Google Ads and DV360 — went live in seven markets this week, and Meltwater confirmed yesterday its feed is now piping verified creator data in via the new API. If you run a conference that sits between creators and brands, this is the plumbing changing underneath you.

OpenAI releases an open-weight PII redactor — runs on your machine.

A small but pointed drop: OpenAI this week open-weighted a model whose only job is to find and strip personally identifiable information from text, designed to run locally so sensitive material never leaves the device. It's a meaningful concession to the "don't send it to the cloud" crowd, and a ready-made primitive for anyone building creator tools with private data.

Figma Make now lives on your phone.

As of April 22, Figma Make — the prompt-to-prototype tool — is inside the mobile app on iOS and Android. You can preview a Make with real gestures and constraints, and ship a review link from the phone you already had in your hand. For small teams iterating live in a meeting, this closes a loop that used to require everyone opening a laptop.

Google's Cloud Next pitch: build the agent, then inbox it.

At Cloud Next this week Google unveiled Workspace Studio, a no-code agent builder; a Model Garden now hosting 200-plus models including Claude; and v1.0 of the A2A agent-to-agent protocol, already in production at 150 organizations. Meta AI, powered by Muse Spark, crashed into the App Store top 5.

After Meta launched its Muse Spark model on April 8, the Meta AI app climbed from #57 to #5 on iOS in a single day, with installs up 87% day-over-day. The model is multimodal, can spawn subagents, and handles visual coding — mini-games and websites from a prompt. Android, notably, barely moved.

Privacy · Law

Congress reaches (again) for a single federal privacy law.

On April 22 the House Energy & Commerce Committee introduced the SECURE Data Act — a bid to replace the patchwork of state privacy statutes with one federal framework. Previous federal attempts have died on the floor; this one arrives as Alabama joins the state list and Kentucky moves to expand sensitive-data coverage, making the patchwork argument harder to wave away.

Privacy · Litigation

Wyze sued for allegedly handing visitor data to TikTok and Meta.

A Washington federal suit filed this week alleges that home-security company Wyze tracked people who visited its website and shared that activity with TikTok and Meta — even when visitors rejected all nonessential cookies. The filing reads as the next chapter of the consent-banner reckoning: "reject all" isn't a defense if it was never honored.

Science · Environment

A humble seed learns to trap microplastics.

A newly-published paper this month shows moringa seeds — yes, the ones people already stir into smoothies — release a compound that causes suspended microplastics to clump, making them simple to filter out. It's early work, but notable for being a low-tech, plant-based answer to a problem that usually invites very expensive hardware.
