Astronomers confirm the first atmosphere on a rocky, habitable-zone planet

A Harvard-led team detected helium escaping the upper atmosphere of LHS 1140b, a super-Earth 48 light-years away — proof the planet has held onto a real atmosphere for over three billion years. It's the strongest evidence yet that a rocky world orbiting in another star's habitable zone can hang onto air.
