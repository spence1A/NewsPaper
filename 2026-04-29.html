A study published in Science Advances on April 15 found that the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) — the ocean current system that regulates temperatures across Europe and North America — could weaken by 42–58% by the end of the century. A weakened AMOC would cause severe weather disruption, sea level rise along the U.S. East Coast, and agricultural failures across Europe.
