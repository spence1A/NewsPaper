Space Science

Curiosity Rover Finds 7 New Organic Molecules on Mars

NASA's Curiosity rover announced the detection of seven previously unknown organic molecules in Martian soil on April 21st, including naphthalene, benzothiophene, and trimethylbenzene — complex carbon-based compounds that are building blocks of life as we know it. While the finding doesn't confirm biology, it significantly raises the probability that Mars once harbored conditions hospitable to life, and gives astrobiologists a fresh set of targets for sample-return missions. The discovery marks the most significant organic chemistry find on Mars to date.

Atlantic Ocean Current Could Weaken 58% by 2100, New Study Warns

A study published in Science Advances on April 15th projects that the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation — the ocean conveyor belt that moderates climate across Europe and North America — is likely to weaken by 42–58% by 2100, far exceeding previous estimates. The most realistic climate models in the study assign a high probability to eventual collapse, which would trigger dramatic temperature shifts, altered rainfall patterns, and rising sea levels along the U.S. East Coast. The research team calls it "the most alarming AMOC projection published to date."
