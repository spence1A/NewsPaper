Science · June

T. rex may have grown for 40 years

A new analysis of 17 tyrannosaur fossils suggests T. rex was a far slower grower than long assumed, likely taking around 40 years to reach full size. The finding reshapes how paleontologists model the predator's metabolism and life history — and how quickly the giant reached its terrifying adult dimensions.

Butterflies that barely seem to age

Researchers report that Heliconius butterflies have evolved an extraordinary lifespan — living several times longer than closely related species, with some showing little sign of physical decline as they age. The work offers a striking natural model for studying the biology of slowed aging.
