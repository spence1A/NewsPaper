A federal US privacy law just got a real bill.

On April 22, the House Energy & Commerce Committee introduced the SECURE Data Act, which would replace the state-by-state privacy patchwork with one federal standard. It requires opt-in consent for sensitive data (health, geolocation) and parental consent for teens 13–15. If it passes, every US privacy compliance regime built since 2018 gets rewritten.

Climate Science

The Atlantic's conveyor belt is weakening faster than we thought.

A study published April 15 in Science Advances projects the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC) will weaken 42–58% by 2100 — significantly more than prior estimates. The most realistic models in the analysis indicate a high probability of eventual collapse. AMOC is the system that, among other things, keeps Northern Europe livable; this is not a small revision.
