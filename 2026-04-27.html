Space · Astrobiology · Apr 24

Mars just gave up seven new molecules.

Curiosity's analysis of "Mary Anning 3" — a clay-rich rock drilled on Mount Sharp — turned up 21 carbon-containing molecules, seven detected on Mars for the first time. Among them: a nitrogen heterocycle (a precursor to RNA/DNA) and benzothiophene, a sulfur-bearing compound found in many meteorites. The paper, in Nature Communications, doesn't prove biology — but it confirms ancient Mars had the chemistry to support it.

Vercel breached. The entry point was an AI tool.

Attackers compromised Context.ai — a third-party AI tool used by a Vercel employee — pivoted into the employee's Google Workspace account, then into Vercel itself, where they decrypted non-sensitive environment variables for a subset of customers. Vercel has notified affected accounts and recommended credential rotation. A "ShinyHunters" lookalike is selling stolen data for $2M on BreachForums. The supply-chain pattern (AI vendor → SSO → production) is the story.

Privacy · US Policy · Apr 22

A federal privacy bill, finally.

House Republicans introduced the SECURE Data Act, which would replace the state-by-state patchwork of consumer privacy laws (CCPA, VCDPA, etc.) with a single federal standard. It mandates opt-in consent for sensitive data — health, geolocation — and requires parental consent before collecting personal data from anyone aged 13–15. The familiar fight ahead: preemption of stronger state laws like California's CCPA. Watch the markup.
