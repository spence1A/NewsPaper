Scientists Crack Solar Desalination With Laser-Etched Metal — No Brine, No Additives

A study published May 27 in Light: Science & Applications described a solar-thermal desalination method producing fresh water from ocean water without brine discharge or chemical additives — two of the biggest barriers to scaling desalination. The innovation: femtosecond laser pulses that etch metal surfaces into a "superwicking" texture drawing water upward through capillary action while concentrating solar heat with exceptional efficiency. Standard reverse-osmosis plants produce roughly 1.5 liters of brine per liter of fresh water, damaging marine ecosystems. This passive, solar-only process eliminates both the brine and the energy cost simultaneously, with the laser-etching step potentially manufacturable at industrial scale.
