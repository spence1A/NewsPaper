A massive breach of the Canvas learning management system leaked nearly 280 million records — covering faculty, staff, and students across 8,809 colleges, online learning platforms, and school districts. The incident ranks among the largest breaches of an educational platform ever recorded and raises sweeping questions about the security posture of ed-tech providers. Privacy & Security

Hackers Stole Biometric Fingerprint Scans From New York's Hospital System

New York's public healthcare network disclosed this week that hackers exfiltrated personal health data and biometric scans — including patients' fingerprints — in one of the largest recorded breaches of 2026. The intrusion began in November 2025, persisted through February 2026, and was traced to an unnamed third-party vendor. The breach is especially alarming because biometric data is irreplaceable: unlike passwords or card numbers, fingerprints cannot be changed once they have been compromised and leaked.

Physics

Scientists Claim Record-Breaking Superconductivity at Atmospheric Pressure

A study published this month claims a copper-based compound achieved superconductivity at the highest temperature ever recorded at normal atmospheric pressure, accomplished via a rapid and controlled release of pressure during synthesis. If independently confirmed, the result would be a landmark in condensed-matter physics — materials that conduct electricity with zero resistance at room conditions could eventually revolutionize energy grids, MRI machines, quantum computers, and high-speed transportation. Independent replication attempts are underway.

Marine Biology

A Tiny Blue Octopus Found 6,000 Feet Deep Near the Galápagos Is an Entirely New Species

Scientists have officially identified a small, vivid blue octopus discovered nearly 6,000 feet beneath the Pacific Ocean near the Galápagos Islands as a brand-new species — one never previously catalogued by science. Encountered during a deep-sea expedition and confirmed through genetic and morphological analysis, the creature's striking coloration and extreme-depth habitat make it one of the more remarkable cephalopod discoveries in recent years. It is also a timely reminder that vast portions of the ocean floor remain virtually unexplored.
